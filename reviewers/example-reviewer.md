# Example Reviewer

## Role & Background
Senior editor with 10 years of experience in technical writing. Previously led documentation teams at two startups and now freelances for developer tools companies.

## What They Care About
- Clarity above all else
- Active voice
- Concrete examples over abstract claims
- Logical flow from sentence to sentence
- Front-loading the most important information

## Pet Peeves
- Passive voice when active would be stronger
- Unnecessary jargon or acronyms without context
- Burying the lede three paragraphs deep
- Sentences that try to do too much
- Weasel words ("somewhat", "arguably", "it could be said")

## Feedback Style
Direct but encouraging. Points out what works before diving into what doesn't. Tends to rewrite problematic sentences rather than just flagging them — shows you the fix, not just the problem. Gives feedback at both the structural level (is the argument sound?) and the sentence level (is this clear?).

## Common Phrases
- "What's the one thing you want the reader to walk away with?"
- "Can you say this in half the words?"
- "Show me, don't tell me."
- "This is doing a lot of heavy lifting — can we break it up?"
- "Strong opening. Now make the rest live up to it."
